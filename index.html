The Problem

Hate speech, duplicate question and unnecessary roughnesss lower the quality of conversations on the internet.
Especially on developer discussion platforms this results in many non deferential conversations missing their goal.

Don't want to read? Watch the Video below!

What the current Question & Answer culture looks like:

Duplicate Topics

A question has already been asked several times.

Incomprehensible

Bad description and/or insufficent details about the problem.

Dumb Questions

The topic creator didn't spend own time in researching.

No Reward for Answers

Time spent solving a problem is not equitably rewarded.

Rough Conversations

Losing patience results in hate speech often towards newcomers.

Arbitrary Moderation

Closing topics or banning accounts without valid reasons.

Unnecessary Discussions

No focus on solving the actual problem.

Long Answer Time

Often it takes days to receive a correct answer.

Outdated answers

Many existing answers are not state-of-the-art.

The Project Goal

DVN introduces a rewarding system for deferential and constructive conversations between developers.
The Developer Network project develops a Q&A portal with an integrated DVN Token tip system.


Watch the video of our vision:

The rewarding Q&A Portal:

A questioner will be able to set a DVN tip reward on his question as shown in the mockup. Whoever gives the best and detailed answer (determined by the questioner and the community) receives the tip.

Profit over Time:

If someone has a question that already has been answered before, he can tip the answer. The person who delivered the helpful answer and the person who initially created the question both receive 2/5 of the tip.

Supporting new Users:

New users will receive an initial registration bonus in DVN out of the platform pool. To keep the pool up, a share of a tip will go into the pool.

Preventing duplicate questions:

We will setup an intuitive search function to prevent duplicate questions. Already created questions should receive the whole attention to possibly receive more and more tips over time.


A new form of Q&A

Receiving compensation for good questions and well-given answers brings up a new constructive form of communication.
No more unnecessary discussions and no dumb questions.
